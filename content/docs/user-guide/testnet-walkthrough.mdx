This walkthrough takes you through the complete Aeternum lifecycle on Sepolia testnet — from connecting your wallet all the way to watching Chainlink automatically transfer your ETH to a backup address. It uses a **5-minute inactivity timer** so you can observe the full recovery cycle in a single session rather than waiting months.

<Callout type="info" title="Sepolia testnet — no real ETH">
  Everything in this walkthrough uses Sepolia test ETH, which has no monetary value. The contract address on Sepolia is `0x33f81c1199732094744ee2efabe9adf8c4eff0db`. You can verify all transactions at [Sepolia Etherscan](https://sepolia.etherscan.io/address/0x33f81c1199732094744ee2efabe9adf8c4eff0db).
</Callout>

---

## What You Need

Before starting, prepare the following:

- **Browser wallet** — MetaMask or any RainbowKit-supported wallet
- **Sepolia ETH** — at least 0.05 SepoliaETH (get it free from [Google Cloud Web3](https://cloud.google.com/application/web3/faucet/ethereum/sepolia))
- **A second Ethereum address** — your "backup address" for this test. This can be a fresh MetaMask account, another wallet you control, or any address you can verify on Etherscan afterwards. You do not need Sepolia ETH in the backup address.

## The Walkthrough

<StepList>
  <StepItem step={1} title="Get Sepolia ETH">
    Open [Google Cloud Web3](https://cloud.google.com/application/web3/faucet/ethereum/sepolia) in a new tab. Connect your wallet and switch to the Sepolia network in the faucet interface. Request Sepolia ETH — the faucet typically delivers 0.1 Sepolia ETH per claim. Wait for the transaction to confirm (usually under 30 seconds).

    Verify your balance in MetaMask or on [sepolia.etherscan.io/address/YOUR_ADDRESS](https://sepolia.etherscan.io). You should see at least 0.05 SepoliaETH.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={2} title="Open the Aeternum app and connect">
    Navigate to [aeternumvault.xyz](https://www.aeternumvault.xyz). Click **Connect Wallet** in the top-right corner. Select your wallet from the RainbowKit modal and approve the connection.

    If prompted to switch networks, confirm the switch to **Sepolia**. Your truncated address now appears in the header.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={3} title="Register your vault with a 5-minute timer">
    Click **Register** on the vault dashboard. The registration modal opens.

    **Backup address:** enter the second Ethereum address you prepared. This is where your Sepolia ETH will be sent when recovery triggers.

    **Inactivity period:** enter `300` seconds (5 minutes). This is far below the mainnet minimum of 180 days — Sepolia testnet allows short timers specifically for testing this flow.

    Leave the optional deposit field empty for now — you will deposit separately in the next step.

    Click **Register** and confirm the transaction in your wallet. Wait for the transaction to confirm (~15 seconds on Sepolia). Your vault is now live and the countdown timer appears on the dashboard.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={4} title="Deposit Sepolia ETH into your vault">
    Click **Deposit** on the dashboard. Enter `0.01` in the ETH field. Confirm the transaction.

    After confirmation, your vault balance shows `0.01 SepoliaETH` and the countdown timer resets to 5 minutes. Chainlink Automation is now watching your vault — as soon as 5 minutes of silence elapses, it will trigger recovery.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={5} title="Do nothing — let the timer expire">
    Wait **at least 5 minutes** without interacting with your vault. Do not click Ping, do not send any transactions from your connected wallet to the vault contract.

    You can watch the countdown timer on the dashboard tick down to zero. Once it reaches zero, your vault becomes eligible for recovery. The Chainlink node calls `checkUpkeep` every 12 seconds — it will detect your vault as due on its next scan.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={6} title="Watch Chainlink execute the recovery">
    Within a minute of the timer expiring, Chainlink's `performUpkeep` transaction will appear on-chain. You can observe it in two ways:

    **On the dashboard:** your vault balance drops to zero, the countdown timer disappears, and a "Recovery executed" entry appears in the vault history feed.

    **On Sepolia Etherscan:** open your vault owner address on [sepolia.etherscan.io](https://sepolia.etherscan.io) and check the "Internal Transactions" tab. You will see an outgoing transfer from the AeternumVault contract address (`0x33f81c1199732094744ee2efabe9adf8c4eff0db`) to your backup address.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={7} title="Confirm the backup address received the ETH">
    Open your backup address on [sepolia.etherscan.io](https://sepolia.etherscan.io). Under the **Internal Txns** tab, you should see a credit of `0.01 SepoliaETH` from the AeternumVault contract. This confirms end-to-end recovery worked correctly.

    The `RecoveryExecuted` event is also logged on the contract's event tab — search for the AeternumVault contract address and filter by `RecoveryExecuted` to see the exact log.
  </StepItem>
</StepList>

## Re-Register and Test Again

After recovery, your vault is deregistered. Your backup address holds the ETH. To run the cycle again:

1. If needed, transfer some Sepolia ETH back to your primary wallet
2. Click **Register** on the dashboard — you can re-register with the same or a different backup address
3. Deposit ETH and repeat

<Callout type="tip" title="Test a Ping reset">
  Try the recovery cycle a second time but this time click **Ping** when the timer reaches 2 minutes. The countdown resets to 5 minutes again. This demonstrates that any interaction — including a cost-minimal ping — completely prevents recovery from firing.
</Callout>

## Testing the Failure Path

If you want to verify the failed recovery handling:

1. Register with a backup address that is a contract that rejects ETH (a contract with `receive() external payable { revert(); }`)
2. Deposit ETH and wait for recovery to trigger
3. The contract will attempt the transfer, fail, restore your balance, increment the retry counter, and emit `RecoveryFailed`
4. After three failures, the vault is abandoned and `RecoveryAbandoned` is emitted
5. Your ETH is still accessible via `withdrawAll()` even after abandonment

This path is easier to observe in a local Foundry test than on Sepolia, where deploying a rejection contract requires additional setup.

## What's Different on Mainnet

Everything you experienced on Sepolia works identically on mainnet with two differences:

- **Timer minimum is 180 days** — you cannot set a 5-minute timer on mainnet. The shortest allowed period is `15,552,000` seconds.
- **Chainlink costs real LINK** — each `performUpkeep` call deducts LINK from the upkeep balance. The Aeternum Labs-maintained upkeep covers this cost; individual users pay no LINK fees.
