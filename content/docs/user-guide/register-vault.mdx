## Before You Register

Registration is a one-time on-chain transaction that creates your vault. Before you call it, two decisions matter — and they are worth thinking through carefully, because they determine how your backup plan actually works.

---

## Decision 1: Your Backup Address

The backup address is the Ethereum wallet that receives your ETH if recovery triggers. Choose it carefully.

**What makes a good backup address:**
- A wallet you genuinely control independently from your primary wallet (separate seed phrase, separate device)
- A hardware wallet in cold storage — air-gapped and unlikely to be compromised at the same time as your primary
- A trusted family member's wallet address, if your goal is inheritance
- A multisig wallet shared with people you trust

**What to avoid:**
- Your own primary wallet as the backup — if you lose your primary, you lose both
- An exchange deposit address — exchanges can reject ETH transfers from smart contracts or change deposit addresses
- A wallet you have not verified recently — make sure you still control the backup address before registering

<Callout type="warning" title="Test your backup address first">
  Before registering with a backup address on mainnet, verify you can receive ETH at that address. Send a small test transaction from a different wallet and confirm receipt. If your backup is a smart contract (like a multisig or a Safe), also confirm it is configured to accept plain ETH — a contract that reverts on `receive()` will cause recovery to fail up to three times before your vault is abandoned.
</Callout>

**Technical requirement:** the backup address cannot be:
- The zero address (`0x000...000`)
- Your own registered address (the wallet calling `register()`)
- An address that has been permanently blocked due to three consecutive failed recovery attempts

---

## Decision 2: Your Inactivity Period

The inactivity period is the length of silence that triggers recovery. It must be between **180 days** (mainnet minimum) and **3,650 days** (about 10 years).

The right period depends on how you actually use crypto:

| Your situation | Suggested period |
| --- | --- |
| Active DeFi user — transacts weekly | 365 days |
| Occasional user — checks in a few times per year | 180–270 days |
| Long-term holder — buys and holds for years | 365–730 days |
| Cold storage vault — rarely touched | 1,095–1,825 days (3–5 years) |

Remember that **any interaction with your vault resets the timer** — deposits, sends, withdrawals, pings, and config updates all count. If you genuinely interact with Ethereum every few months, a 180-day timer is fine. If you might go 18 months without needing to touch your funds, choose at least 2 years.

<Callout type="tip">
  When in doubt, choose longer. A 365-day period with an annual `ping()` reminder in your calendar is safer than a 180-day period you might miss during a busy stretch of life.
</Callout>

## Registering on the Dashboard

<StepList>
  <StepItem step={1} title="Open the Register modal">
    Connect your wallet and ensure you are on Sepolia. Click the **Register** button in the vault dashboard. A modal opens asking for your backup address and inactivity period.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={2} title="Enter your backup address">
    Paste the full Ethereum address of your chosen backup wallet. The form validates the address format in real time. If the address is your own connected address or a previously abandoned backup address, the form will show an error before you can submit.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={3} title="Set your inactivity period">
    Enter the inactivity period in days (the app converts to seconds internally). On Sepolia testnet, you can enter a very short period (e.g. `0.003472` days = 5 minutes = 300 seconds) to test the recovery flow quickly.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={4} title="Optionally add an initial deposit">
    You can deposit ETH at the same time as registration by entering an ETH amount. This is optional — your vault can be registered with a zero balance and funded later via `deposit()`. A vault with zero balance is registered and active but recovery will not trigger (there is nothing to recover).
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={5} title="Confirm the transaction">
    Click **Register** in the modal. Your wallet prompts you to sign and broadcast the transaction. The `register()` call costs approximately 150,000–200,000 gas, depending on whether you are also depositing ETH.
  </StepItem>

  <StepItem step={6} title="Vault is live">
    Once the transaction confirms (typically 12–30 seconds on Sepolia), your vault dashboard updates. You will see your balance, backup address, and the recovery countdown timer. Chainlink Automation begins monitoring your vault immediately.
  </StepItem>
</StepList>

## After Registration

Once registered, you can update any setting at any time while your vault is active:

- **Change your backup address** → Update Config in the sidebar → enter the new address
- **Change your inactivity period** → Update Config → enter the new period in days
- **Add more ETH** → Deposit button on the dashboard
- **Exit entirely** → Cancel Recovery in the sidebar — this withdraws your balance and deregisters your vault

Each configuration update resets your inactivity timer, confirming that you are alive and in control. See [Vault Actions](/docs/user-guide/vault-actions) for a full breakdown of every available operation.
